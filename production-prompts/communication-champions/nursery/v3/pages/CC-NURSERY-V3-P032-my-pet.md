## 6. Educational Engine

- Objective: Talk about a pet or an animal the child would like to care for.
- Primary activity: Draw a pet or an animal you would care for, then choose one care action: feed, give water, keep safe.
- Secondary activity: One large drawing frame and one care-choice strip.
- Observable evidence: Child depicts an animal and names one responsible care action.
- Developmental response modes: pointing, gesture, one word, short phrase, colouring, tracing, drawing, matching, or adult-supported dictation.

## 7. Teaching Engine

- Facilitation: Explain that children may draw an imagined pet and adults are responsible for safe animal care.
- Model once, invite participation, pause for processing, scaffold through gesture/choice/model language, and affirm effort without shame.
- Teacher–student interaction must be visible, purposeful, inclusive, and connected to the exact learning objective.
- Assessment evidence: Child depicts an animal and names one responsible care action.

## 8. Parent Partnership Engine
