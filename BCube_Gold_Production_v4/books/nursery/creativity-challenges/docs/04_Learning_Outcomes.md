# Learning Outcomes

By the end of the book, a Nursery learner should be able to:

## Imagine
- Suggest more than one possible use, ending or visual idea.
- Complete an unfinished picture using a personally chosen idea.
- Invent a simple character, object or scene from familiar shapes.

## Create
- Make controlled marks, lines, shapes and colour areas.
- Combine visual parts into a recognisable whole.
- Follow a simple two-step creative activity with adult support.

## Experiment
- Try a different colour, arrangement, shape or solution.
- Notice that creative activities can have multiple valid answers.
- Revise or add to an idea rather than stopping after the first attempt.

## Express
- Name or describe a creation using simple words or phrases.
- Point out a favourite part and explain a basic choice.
- Share work with growing confidence.

## Supporting development
- Improved pencil and crayon control
- Improved visual discrimination and spatial awareness
- Increased descriptive vocabulary
- Greater persistence, choice-making and task confidence
