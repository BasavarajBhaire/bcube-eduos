# Communication Champions — P010: My Family

**Page ID:** CC-NURSERY-V3-P010  
**Book:** Communication Champions  
**Level:** Nursery (3+)  
**Status:** GOLD SPECIFICATION APPROVED  

## Learning objective

I can talk about the people who care for me and are part of my family.

## Observable outcomes

The child can:

- name one or more people in their family;
- use a short relationship word or caregiver term;
- share one simple family activity;
- understand that families can look different;
- speak about family members with confidence and respect.

## Locked visible wording

- Page title: **My Family**
- Activity 1: **Point to the people who are in your family.**
- Activity 2: **Draw the people who are part of your family.**
- Activity 3: **My family loves to...**
- Reward text: **Family Star**
- Reward sentence: **My family is special.**

## Page composition

### Header

### Hero illustration

Show the approved Star mascot sitting with one happy Nursery child while they look at a family photo album. The album may show caring adults, siblings, grandparents, or another important family member. Keep the scene inclusive and avoid presenting one family structure as the only correct model. Use a clean white background with minimal pastel accents and generous whitespace.

### Activity 1 — Who is in my family?

Provide six large, clearly separated picture cards representing:

- parent or caregiver;
- grandparent;
- brother or sister;
- baby;
- another caring family member;
- pet as an optional discussion prompt.

Instruction: **Point to the people who are in your family.**

Do not require the child to select every card.

### Activity 2 — Draw my family

Provide one large rounded drawing frame with thick border and ample space for Nursery-level drawing.

Instruction: **Draw the people who are part of your family.**

### Activity 3 — My family story

Provide one large speech bubble.

Prompt: **My family loves to...**

The teacher or parent records the child’s exact words. This is an oral-language activity, not a writing requirement for the child.

### Reward area

Small Star badge with the text **Family Star** and the sentence **My family is special.** Include space for one sticker, smiley, or stamp.

## Teacher guidance

- Invite children to describe the people who care for them.
- Accept every family structure naturally and respectfully.
- Encourage short phrases such as "This is my grandma" or "I play with my brother."
- Never compare families or require a child to explain an absent family member.
- Record the child’s words without correcting personal names or family terms unnecessarily.

## Parent connection

- Look at one family photograph together.
- Ask the child to name the people shown.
- Invite one short story about a family activity.
- Celebrate family traditions and caring relationships.
