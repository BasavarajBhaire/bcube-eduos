## Learning objective
Talk about a pet or an animal the child would like to care for.

## Exact visible wording
Draw my pet. My pet is a ______. I can help by ______.

## Child action
Draw a pet or an animal you would care for, then choose one care action: feed, give water, keep safe.

## Observable evidence
Child depicts an animal and names one responsible care action.

## Teacher move
Explain that children may draw an imagined pet and adults are responsible for safe animal care.

### Teacher question
- How would you care for this animal?

## Parent connection
Observe an animal safely and discuss one thing it needs; do not approach unfamiliar animals.

## Locked composition
Large drawing frame with three small care icons—food bowl, water bowl, safe shelter—and Star beside the frame.

## Exact response space
One large drawing frame and one care-choice strip.

## Page-specific prohibition
No child alone with dangerous animal, pet purchase message, cage stereotype or completed drawing.
