# Communication Champions — P009: My Birthday

**Page ID:** CC-NURSERY-V3-P009  
**Book:** Communication Champions  
**Level:** Nursery (3+)  
**Status:** GOLD SPECIFICATION APPROVED  

## Learning objective

I understand that my birthday is a special day and I can talk about how I celebrate it with people who care for me.

## Observable outcomes

The child can:

- say that a birthday is a special day;
- talk about one birthday memory or wish;
- identify people who may celebrate with them;
- use a short phrase such as "My birthday is special";
- practise gratitude by saying "Thank you".

## Locked visible wording

- Page title: **My Birthday**
- Main sentence: **My birthday is a special day.**
- Activity 1: **Colour and decorate the birthday cake.**
- Activity 2: **Circle the people who celebrate with you.**
- Activity 3: **On my birthday I would like to...**
- Reward text: **Birthday Star!**

## Page composition

### Header

- Official BCube logo at top left.

- Small Star mascot at top right holding one balloon.
- Interior page number at bottom right only.

### Hero illustration

Show one Nursery child celebrating beside the approved Star mascot and a simple birthday cake with three candles. Include a small, inclusive group of caring family members or friends. Keep the child and cake dominant. Use a clean white background with minimal pastel accents. Do not overload the scene with balloons, gifts, confetti, or decorative clutter.

### Activity 1 — Decorate my cake

Provide one large cake outline with broad colouring areas and thick rounded outlines.

Instruction: **Colour and decorate the birthday cake.**

### Activity 2 — Who celebrates with me?

Provide six large, clearly separated picture cards representing:

- parent or caregiver;
- grandparent;
- sibling;
- friend;
- teacher;
- other caring family member.

Instruction: **Circle the people who celebrate with you.**

Do not imply that every child has the same family structure.

### Activity 3 — My birthday wish

Provide one large speech cloud.

Prompt: **On my birthday I would like to...**

The teacher or parent records the child’s words. This is an oral-language activity, not a writing requirement for the child.

### Reward area

Small Star badge with the text **Birthday Star!** and space for one sticker, smiley, or stamp.

## Teacher guidance

- Ask what makes a birthday feel special.
- Invite the child to name one person who celebrates with them.
- Encourage one short sentence about a birthday memory or wish.
- Model gratitude with "Thank you".
- Do not require the child to memorise or write a date.

## Parent connection

- Look at one birthday photograph together.
- Ask who was present and what the child remembers.
- Invite the child to say one birthday wish.
- Practise saying "Thank you" to family and friends.
