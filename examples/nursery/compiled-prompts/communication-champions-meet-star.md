## Final image-generation prompt

Create a complete A4 portrait preschool workbook page for **BCube Future Academy**, trim size 210 × 297 mm, high-resolution 300 DPI, print-ready, with 3 mm bleed, a clean white background with very light pastel accents, 10 mm safe margins and a protected inside binding margin. Place the official BCube logo at the top left, the exact title **“Meet Star”** centred clearly at the top, and page number **5** at the bottom right. Do not place a Nursery age badge on this interior page. Keep every face, hand, learning object, line of text and child activity space safely inside the live area.

Design for Nursery children aged 3+. Use large recognisable shapes, thick clean rounded outlines, friendly natural expressions, generous white space and minimal visual complexity. Maintain approximately 75% visual content and 25% text. The page must teach one clear communication action: greeting someone with a wave and a friendly hello.

Make Star the dominant focal point, occupying about 45–50% of the main illustration area. Star is a bright yellow five-point mascot with softly rounded points, large expressive eyes, rosy cheeks, a friendly smile, blue shoes and a small blue cape. Star stands upright near the centre, facing the children, with one arm raised in a clear, natural wave. Star looks warm, welcoming and encouraging—not hyperactive, decorative or distracting.

Show a friendly preschool teacher kneeling at child eye level slightly to the left of Star. The teacher smiles naturally and models a gentle wave with an open hand. Show two diverse nursery children beside the teacher, looking toward Star and waving back. Their body language should communicate curiosity, comfort and growing confidence. Keep all hands anatomically correct with five fingers where visible. The teacher must actively support the greeting rather than standing passively.

Use a simple preschool welcome corner as the environment: a small low bookshelf, one soft floor mat and one simple wall shape or picture, all in light pastel colours. Keep the background sparse and secondary. Do not add balloons, decorative stars, toys or classroom objects that do not support the greeting activity.

Create a clear eye-flow sequence: exact page title at the top, Star’s smiling face and waving hand, teacher modelling the wave, children responding, then the child activity area. Reserve a large uncluttered lower-right area containing one broad dotted curved path that the child can trace to practise the motion of a wave. Do not allow any character, prop, border or speech bubble to overlap this activity space.

Add the following exact visible text using large, rounded, highly legible typography during page layout:

- Title: **Meet Star**
- Child instruction: **Wave and say, ‘Hello, Star!’**
- Teacher support label: **Teacher says: Hello, Star!**
- Parent extension label: **Try a friendly hello at home.**

Keep the child instruction prominent and short. Put the teacher support in a compact teacher box near the lower-left side, and the parent extension in a smaller non-dominant box above the footer. Do not generate any other words, labels or speech text.

The learning objective is for the child to recognise Star as a friendly learning companion and respond to a simple greeting. The visible success evidence is that the child waves, attempts the greeting verbally or uses another comfortable greeting gesture. The page must feel safe for shy children; never pressure every child to speak aloud.

Use warm pastel colours, strong figure-ground separation, soft minimal shadows and premium preschool publishing quality. The teacher, children and Star must have clean silhouettes and consistent scale. Keep the composition balanced and calm.

Negative constraints: no photorealism, no dark or fully coloured background, no harsh shadows, no crowded classroom, no random decorative icons, no watermarks, no signatures, no unrelated logos, no gibberish, no extra text, no distorted anatomy, no extra fingers, no missing limbs, no cropped faces or hands, no unsafe action, no blocked tracing area and no unresolved placeholders or references to external modules.

Final validation requirement: the finished page must be self-contained, publication-ready, page-specific, educationally clear and visually understandable without any additional instructions.
